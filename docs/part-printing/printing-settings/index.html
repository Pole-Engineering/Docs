How the Print Settings Should be and Which Materials to Use?

We are suggesting to print the parts with following instructions.

Print Settings​

You can use two different printing settings to print the parts;

Ultra Durable​

We recommend this settings for ultra durability. Its strong but takes longer to print.​

SettingsValues
Layer Height0.2mm
Line Width0.4mm
Wall Count5
Top & Bottom Layer Count5
Infill PatternHoneycomb
Infill Density%30

I Mean... It'll Work but Significantly Weaker​

These settings suitable for impatients.​

SettingsValues
Layer Height0.28mm
Line Width0.4mm
Wall Count3
Top & Bottom Layer Count3
Infill PatternGyroid, Cubic, Grid
Infill Density%30

How the Printing Orientation Should be?​

Every STLs that you will download from Github are exported with right printing orientation so dont need the change the order.

## Material

ABS​

  • You need to print most of the parts with ABS material. ABS provides high temperature resistance compared with other common 3D printing materials. ABS's glass temperature is around 105°C (221°F) so its ideal for the parts that exposed to heat such as motor mounts. We highly recommend ABS for the 3D printed parts.

PETG​

  • PETG might be the second best option to print parts out of it. PETG has glass temperature around 85°C (185°F). It's not recommended for enclosed printers.

PLA​

  • PLA is the worst option to use for areas exposed to heat because of the glass temperature. PLA's glass temperature is around 60°C (140°F). But you can still use PLA for panel trims or skirts.
